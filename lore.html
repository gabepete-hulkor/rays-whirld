World Regions

🏙️ Civilized Zones

Urban settings with shops, guilds, law enforcement, and political structures. Clerics and Druids are less effective in extreme urban settings. Technology levels vary widely.

🌲 Wilderness

Rangers and Druids excel here. Animal companions are more common. Rural survival skills are essential. Monsters roam freely.

💀 Post-Apocalyptic Zones

Death worlds at war. Extreme danger. Technology and magic mix in unexpected ways. Clarke's Law and Ray's Whirld Law both apply here.

🪨 Extreme Caverns / Underworld

Vast underground systems. Infravision is key. Unique creatures. The underworld has its own ecology, politics, and civilizations.

🚀 Exo-Planets & Space

Ray's Whirld extends beyond its own surface. Spelljammer-style space travel may be possible. Alien civilizations and technologies.

The Federation of Law

A major lawful organization in Ray's Whirld. Paladins donate 10% of their income to the Federation. Jedi classes receive "Federation of Law Support." The Federation likely maintains order in civilized zones and provides infrastructure for lawful-aligned characters.

The Physics of Ray's Whirld

⚛️ Physics Varies

Ray's Whirld does not have consistent physics. Depending on location:

  • The world appears "flat" in some regions
  • Space bends — teleportation and dimensional travel may be possible
  • Quantum events happen at macro scale (what happens to individual particles can happen to people)
  • Multiple moons affect tidal forces, magic cycles, and weather

Clarke's Law vs. Ray's Whirld Law

Clarke's Law

"Technology sufficiently advanced resembles Magic."

In Ray's Whirld, this means that high-tech civilizations and items may appear magical to lower-tech cultures.

Ray's Whirld Law

"Advanced Magic may resemble Technology."

The reverse is also true — extremely advanced magic can appear to function like technology. This creates a unique blending of sci-fi and fantasy.
