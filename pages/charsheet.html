Character Creation

Creating a character in Ray's Whirld is a multi-step process. Work with your DM at every stage!

Step-by-Step Creation

Step 1: Who Do You Want to Be?

Choose Class(es) and generate randomly or choose a Template (Archetype). Think of your favorite adventurer from books, movies, games, or your own ideas.

Templates cost Grudge — Worth it?!

Step 2: What Else?

Choose Subclasses, Back Story, Skills, Proficiencies, and Alignment. Work with your DM on all of these.

Step 3: Roll 'Em!

Roll your ability scores using the best options available for your class. Use Grudge to improve bad rolls!

Step 4: Mutations & Specials

Choose 1–5 Mutations (Random or By Template). 1 in 5 is Defective — roll well! Use Grudge. Choose Special Abilities class by class, plus financial and family background.

Step 5: Fix It — Last Chance!

Grudge, Grudge, Grudge. Spend Grudge to fix scores, mutations, proficiencies, and skills. Last chance before play!

Step 6: Equip!

The best you can. Use Grudge as available. Mind your proficiencies and skills — take note!

Names

Basic Name

What you start with. Unfortunate names may earn Grudge (e.g., PickButtKiss, Flemmy HandShake, Upchuck, Snotgas).

Flashy names are free (mostly). Example: Fabian BigThews.

Heroe Name

What you earn and live up to. A good Heroe Name should:

  1. Hint at your powers
  2. Strike fear in your enemies
  3. Look good on Slurpee Cups and other Promotional Materials (ProMat)

"Copyrights apply — don't call yourself Superman!"

Character Sheet Guidelines

📋 Sheet Structure

Each character sheet is different, but all share some basics:

  • Page 1 (Self): Name, Heroe Name, Species, Classes & Levels, Alignment, Appearance, HP, All Scores, Special Abilities, Mutations, Saving Throws, Combat Modifiers (Math Section — don't panic!)
  • Equipment Pages: Outfit, Kits, Weapons, Wands, Potions, Vehicles, etc.
  • Character Log: Separate section or notebook — "Today My Adventure Began…" Track victories and defeats for XP.

Leaving Home

Upon coming of age (humans approximately 15 years old), the hero leaves home. Possible reasons:

  • To prove self ("I'll Show Them!")
  • To become rich & famous
  • Monsters destroyed your town and you are the only one left
  • Exiled (Chaotic) or Assigned Duty (Lawful)
  • Sold into slavery

Your backstory will be developed with your DM.

A Ray's Whirld character sheet covers everything about your hero in three big areas: About Me (who you are), What I Can Do (your abilities in play), and What I Have (your gear and resources). Every section below maps to real game mechanics.

📊 Ability Scores

Your six core attributes (3-18+), each with a modifier:

  • STR (Strength) - Physical power, melee damage
  • DEX (Dexterity) - Agility, AC, ranged attacks
  • CON (Constitution) - Health, stamina, resilience
  • INT (Intelligence) - Learning, knowledge, Mage spells
  • WIS (Wisdom) - Perception, willpower, Cleric spells
  • CHA (Charisma) - Leadership, persuasion, Bard spells
