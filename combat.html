Combat

Developed circa 1980 → D20 Combat System. All combat in Ray's Whirld uses a sophisticated Attack vs. Defence comparison system with 2d20 rolls.

Combat Overview

Attack and Defence each have a numerical value based on Strength, Dexterity, Class, and Level, plus bonuses from weapons, proficiency, armor class, and multi-strike. The difference between Atk and Def gives a bonus to the higher number.

📋 Each Round
Roll 2d20 (high numbers win). The attacker calls the low die with bonus (if applicable). The defender responds. Then the attacker calls the high die and the defender responds.

⚠ Fumble!
If the attacker misses, has both natural dice under 10, AND the defender has both natural dice over 10 — the attacker has fumbled! Roll on the Fumble Table.

Initiative

Initiative is base d6 at Normal Speed. High score wins. Tie = Reroll with Next Higher Die. All bonuses apply. With initiative, a character receives a free Dodge (free action).

Dodge & Block

🏃 Dodge

Requires: Acrobatics skill (+1) AND Gymnastics skill (+1) = +2 Dodge bonus (+4 total with both)

1 Action = 1 Da (Dodge Action). Dodge is divided among all attackers. With initiative, receive a free Dodge (free action). Without initiative — Dodge costs an action.

Dodging stacks up vs. attackers.

🛡️ Block

Requires: An action, by hand or item (blade, staff, shield, etc.)

+1 Per Proficiency and bonus (e.g., Blades II +3 = +5 Block, 2/Round). Only one block max per attack applies. Blocking is divided among attacks.

Bless, Pray, Idol, Wish… also provide bonuses!

Combat Example

Example Round

Attacker: "I have +10 on my dice (2d20). I'm sure to hit!"
Defender: "I'm doomed! Probably. But maybe…"
They roll.
Attacker: "I rolled a 1! That's 1+10=11. Low die call: 11."
Defender: "HaHa! Natural 12! I beat your low die!"
Attacker: "High die: 13 (3+10)."
Defender: "Natural 15! You've fumbled!"
That's why we roll dice. Unexpected is not impossible. That's why you have Grudge.
