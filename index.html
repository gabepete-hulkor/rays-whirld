Introduction

Ray's Whirld is an Extreme Fantasy Sci-fi Game World, drawing ideas from a large variety of games, movies, comics, etc.

Core Tenets of Ray's Whirld

1. Powerful Characters

PCs are made to be powerful and unique. Choose a template or generate randomly. Multi-classing, mutations, special abilities, and superhuman ability scores are all part of the game.

2. Magic is Powerful

Magic is powerful and common — so many spells. Assemble your outfit, custom weapons, maximize world FX, use vehicles, Grudge, and more.

3. Enormously Huge World

Surface area over 30× Earth. Includes extreme caverns & underworld, exo-planets and space, post-apocalyptic Earth/other worlds. Some places are very low in magic and tech — room for anything!

4. Extremely Deadly

Monsters, NPCs (Dracula, Superman, Doc Doom), environmental traps, post-apocalyptic death worlds at war, and more. Be afraid. Check for traps.

5. PCs Are Heroes

With great power comes great challenge and great responsibility. Celebrity Status: agents, fans, haters. Take notes, pay attention.

6. You Can Try Again

How dead are you? Grudge, potions, familiars, partners, start a new character — death doesn't have to be final in Ray's Whirld.

7. Few Limits

Class/level advancement, adventure scope (save the world — again?), extreme equipment, superhuman ability scores (90 Strength? We can do that!) and more.

8. Weirdness

Physics varies. Space bends. "Flat" world. Quantum events on macro scale. How many moons? History and creation myths. Ecology. And more!

9. Start Weak, Advance Quickly

Starting at 1st level: young, weak, poorly equipped — Little League! More levels → more better everything! Pay attention! New powers, increasing attack, defence, and skill rolls. Higher levels mean slower advancement.

10. Math!

"Roll" Playing. Take notes. Use a calculator at high levels. Do the math! Attack numbers and defence numbers determine everything — do not skip the math.

The Grudge System

Grudge are character building or "cheat" points. Every player starts with 3.0 Grudge to build their character.

Starting Amount
3.0 points
Range
–3.0 to +3.0
Die Roll Modifier

Earning Grudge

  • Good role play
  • DM's challenge (quiz or game)
  • Good player actions (not metagame)
  • Bribery (LOL)
  • Taking a penalty or defect
  • Being polite, and more!

Spending Grudge

  • Improve any aspect during character creation
  • Change a die roll during play (±1/10 Grudge)
  • Take a defect or penalty to gain Grudge back
  • Use before rolling to improve your odds
  • Ask the DM: "Can I use Grudge to…?"

The DM (Da Man)

Traditionally called the "Dungeon Master" or "GM." In Ray's Whirld, we say "Da Man" as in "You Da Man!" The DM plays the World (Universe) in which your character acts.

The DM must set up situations of all types and keep track of a nigh-unlimited amount of geography, creatures, situations, and non-player characters (NPCs).

Rule #1
Ask the DM. How? What? When? Why? Where? Listen to the DM. "I might not give the answer that you want…" Ask, ask, ask — and be polite!

  • Slower advancement

Combat

Developed circa 1980 → D20 Combat System. All combat in Ray's Whirld uses a sophisticated Attack vs. Defence comparison system with 2d20 rolls.

Combat Overview

Attack and Defence each have a numerical value based on Strength, Dexterity, Class, and Level, plus bonuses from weapons, proficiency, armor class, and multi-strike. The difference between Atk and Def gives a bonus to the higher number.

📋 Each Round
Roll 2d20 (high numbers win). The attacker calls the low die with bonus (if applicable). The defender responds. Then the attacker calls the high die and the defender responds.

Roll Outcomes

OutcomeResult
Attacker wins both rollsCritical Hit!
Tie: Natural beats ModifiedDefender advantage
Attacker wins 1 rollNon-Critical Hit
Both Natural: TieUse Grudge or Lose & Receive
Attacker loses bothComplete Miss
Attacker misses & both natural dice <10, Defender both natural >10Attacker FUMBLES — roll on Fumble Table!
⚠ Fumble!
If the attacker misses, has both natural dice under 10, AND the defender has both natural dice over 10 — the attacker has fumbled! Roll on the Fumble Table.

Initiative

Initiative is base d6 at Normal Speed. High score wins. Tie = Reroll with Next Higher Die. All bonuses apply. With initiative, a character receives a free Dodge (free action).

Lör Magic System

Elemental magic inspired by the Thomas Covenant series and Avatar: The Last Airbender. Lör is powerful, risky, and alignment-restricted. Get your Wisdom up!

✨ What is Lör?
Lör is a point-based elemental magic system. Unlike traditional spellcasting, Lör uses Lör Points (LP) which are earned through meditation and use, then spent to activate elemental powers. The system is probability-based — your chance of success depends on Wisdom and Level.

Life, Plant, Earth are the best types for enhancement powers. H₂O, Fire, and Air can also enhance but cost more LP (e.g., Incr. Str. Self II = +LorLo per 15LP vs. 10LP for best types).

⚡ First Power
All Lör Workers must have "Lör Sense" as their first power. This is non-negotiable.

Lör Levels (LorLo and LorHi)

LorLo = Lör Level + ½(Wisdom – 15) [round up]
LorHi = LorLo + Dex Bonus (+½ Dex > 15)

These values determine the power magnitude of Lör effects. Example: at Level 1 with 19 Wisdom: LorLo = 1 + 2 = 3.

Using LP — Step by Step

  1. Decide how many LP to spend (up to max per round: Wis × Level, max 50)
  2. Calculate % Chance: LP Used ÷ Lör Level
  3. If you spend ALL remaining LP, you get a bonus roll at max % chance
  4. Roll d100 — compare to % Chance
  5. Any unused % is "banked" for after the next rest
  6. If Lör Level + % rolled > 100 → FUMBLE!

Critical Success

If the roll is 10% or less of the target, it's a Critical Success!

  • At 1st Level: First level characters receive Max Points!
  • Over 1st Level: No division by level for the Lör Increase Roll
📊 LP Gain on Success
LP Gain = (% Target – % Rolled) ÷ Level → Roll that die (min 1 LP)

Example: Rolled 10% vs. 19% target at Level 1 → (19–10)÷1 = 9 → Roll d9(+1) → Gain LP! Advance to Level 2 if trained!

Fumble!

💥 Fumble Condition
Fumble IF: Lör Level + % rolled > 100

Effect: Lose (d4 × Level) Lör Points. This can cripple a high-level Lör user!

Lizardfolk have little interest in money or jewels, and value things primarily based on whether they are useful or edible. They are not inherently evil — simply savage with a hard time fitting into civilized society. They refer to most civilized races as "softskins."

The Physics of Ray's Whirld

⚛️ Physics Varies

Ray's Whirld does not have consistent physics. Depending on location:

  • The world appears "flat" in some regions
  • Space bends — teleportation and dimensional travel may be possible
  • Quantum events happen at macro scale (what happens to individual particles can happen to people)
  • Multiple moons affect tidal forces, magic cycles, and weather

Clarke's Law vs. Ray's Whirld Law

Clarke's Law

"Technology sufficiently advanced resembles Magic."

In Ray's Whirld, this means that high-tech civilizations and items may appear magical to lower-tech cultures.

Ray's Whirld Law

"Advanced Magic may resemble Technology."

The reverse is also true — extremely advanced magic can appear to function like technology. This creates a unique blending of sci-fi and fantasy.

Mutations & Special Abilities

Everyone in Ray's Whirld is descended from mutant stock. Mutations are gifts (and sometimes curses) that make every character truly unique. Choose 1–5 mutations at character creation — 1 in 5 is a Defect. Use Grudge to re-roll or improve.

Mutations are powered by Luck. More Luck = more powerful mutations. Roll randomly or use a template (costs Grudge). Every 5th mutation roll is a Defect — a drawback that balances the power. Roll 1d500 — or let the DM choose.
